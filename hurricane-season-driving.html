Navigating Hurricane Season: Safe Driving Tips & Emergency Kit Essentials

Atlantic hurricane season runs June 1 through November 30, and in Southwest Florida that means sudden downpours, flooded intersections, and the occasional named storm. A little preparation keeps your trip safe and stress-free.

Driving in heavy rain

  • Slow down and increase following distance. Wet pavement plus afternoon storms is the most common hazard — not the hurricanes themselves.
  • Turn on headlights (Florida law requires it when wipers are on).
  • Never drive through standing water. "Turn around, don't drown" — just a foot of moving water can carry a vehicle.
  • Watch for dark intersections. Treat signals out as a four-way stop.

Emergency car kit checklist

  • Bottled water and non-perishable snacks
  • Phone charger and a backup power bank
  • Flashlight and spare batteries
  • Basic first-aid kit
  • Paper map (signal and GPS can fail)
  • Rain poncho and a small towel

If a storm is forecast

Check the National Hurricane Center and local Lee & Charlotte County alerts before any trip. If a watch or warning is issued, keep the tank above half full, avoid coastal and low-lying roads, and don't try to "beat" a storm on the highway.
