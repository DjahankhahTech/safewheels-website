Craft Brews & Local Sips: Fort Myers' Thriving Drink Scene

Why Fort Myers Is a Craft Drink Destination Worth Exploring

When most people think of Southwest Florida, they picture white-sand beaches and turquoise Gulf water — and rightfully so. But Fort Myers has quietly built one of the most exciting craft beverage scenes in the state. Over the past decade, a wave of independent breweries, taprooms, distilleries, and cocktail lounges has taken root here, fueled by a community of passionate locals and a year-round influx of visitors hungry for something more than a chain-restaurant margarita.

Whether you're a craft-beer connoisseur, a cocktail enthusiast, or simply someone who appreciates a cold, locally made drink after a day in the Florida sun, Fort Myers has a glass (or a flight) with your name on it. Best of all, these spots are spread across distinct neighborhoods, making a self-guided sipping tour a genuinely fun way to see the city. Having your own set of wheels — picked up right at RSW or delivered straight to your accommodation — makes hopping between stops effortless.

The Craft Brewery Trail: Taprooms You Shouldn't Miss

Fort Myers' craft beer community is anchored by a handful of standout breweries, each with its own personality.

  • Barrel of Americans Brewing Co. — A relaxed, dog-friendly taproom with a rotating lineup of IPAs, stouts, and Florida-inspired seasonal brews. Their outdoor patio is a favorite spot to settle in for a long afternoon.
  • Point Ybel Brewing Company — Named for the historic lighthouse on Sanibel, this brewery celebrates its SWFL roots in every pour. Look for their coconut-infused ales and island-style lagers that taste like vacation in a glass.
  • Momentum Brewhouse — A newer player on the scene that has quickly earned a loyal following with bold, creative recipes and a lively taproom atmosphere. Their food menu is a cut above the typical brewery fare, too.
  • Bury Me Brewing — A passion project from local brewing veterans, this intimate spot rewards those who seek it out with some of the most thoughtfully crafted small-batch beers in the region.

Many of these breweries host live music, trivia nights, and food truck events on weekends — check their social pages before you go to catch something special.

Sip Beyond Beer: Distilleries & Wine Bars

Not a beer drinker? Fort Myers has you covered with a growing roster of distilleries and wine-focused spots that are every bit as worth your time.

  • Southwest Florida Distillery (Twisted Cypress) — One of Florida's most celebrated craft distilleries, producing small-batch bourbons, gins, and rums using locally sourced ingredients. Their tasting room pours are generous, and the tour of the production floor is genuinely fascinating.
  • The Wine Loft — A sophisticated yet unpretentious wine bar in the heart of downtown Fort Myers with an impressive rotating selection of bottles from around the world. Their knowledgeable staff are happy to guide you toward something new.
  • Pinot's Palette & similar wine-and-paint venues — A uniquely Fort Myers way to enjoy a glass: guided painting classes where the wine flows freely and the results are always entertaining.

The River District: Fort Myers' Cocktail Heartbeat

No sipping tour of Fort Myers is complete without an evening in the historic River District, the city's vibrant downtown core running along the Caloosahatchee River. This walkable stretch is packed with cocktail bars, rooftop lounges, and eclectic restaurants that double as excellent places for a craft drink.

Pull up to the River District — parking is easy on evenings and weekends — and wander at your own pace. Spots worth ducking into include rooftop bars with sweeping river views, speakeasy-style cocktail dens tucked into restored historic buildings, and lively outdoor patios where the warm Florida night air makes every sip taste a little better. The vibe is festive but never overwhelming, and you'll find a mix of tourists, snowbirds, and locals sharing the same barstools.

Pro tip: Thursday evenings often feature Art Walk, when the galleries and shops of the River District open their doors late and the streets come alive with live music and street performers — a perfect backdrop for your evening out.

Planning Your Fort Myers Sipping Tour: Practical Tips

A craft drink tour across multiple stops calls for a little planning to do it safely and comfortably. Here's how to make the most of your day:

  • Designate a driver or use a rideshare app — If you plan to drink at multiple stops, take turns being the designated driver in your group, or lean on Uber and Lyft, which are readily available throughout Fort Myers. Your rental car will be waiting safely wherever you parked it.
  • Start early with the brewery trail — Most taprooms open around noon. Hit two or three in the afternoon, grab food from a food truck or nearby restaurant, and save the River District cocktail bars for the evening when the atmosphere really peaks.
  • Drive between stops, not after — The breweries and the River District are not all in the same neighborhood, so having a rental car for getting from one cluster to the next — before drinking — is genuinely convenient. Arriving at RSW or PGD and having your car delivered means you're mobile from the moment you land.
  • Ask about flight samplers — Nearly every taproom offers tasting flights. They're the best way to explore a broad range of styles without committing to a full pour of something you're unsure about.
  • Check for seasonal specialties — SWFL's craft producers love leaning into local flavors: Key lime, mango, citrus, and even Florida honey show up in seasonal brews and spirits throughout the year.
