Snowbird Season in SWFL: A Newcomer's Driving & Parking Guide

Each winter, Southwest Florida welcomes a wave of "snowbirds" escaping the cold. If it's your first season on the Gulf Coast, a little local know-how makes getting around far less stressful.

Expect seasonal traffic

From roughly January through March, roads are noticeably busier. US-41 (Tamiami Trail) and the bridges between Cape Coral and Fort Myers see the most congestion at midday and around dinnertime. Build in extra time, and consider running errands mid-morning when it's calmer.

Parking tips

  • Beaches fill early. Arrive before 10am for spots at Fort Myers Beach, Sanibel, and Barefoot Beach.
  • Bring small bills or an app. Many beach and downtown lots are paid; some use license-plate kiosks.
  • Downtown districts (Cape Coral's 47th Terrace, Fort Myers River District, Punta Gorda) have garages and lots a block or two off the main strip.

Driving courtesies

Watch for cyclists and pedestrians in beach towns, keep right except to pass, and be patient at the many roundabouts. Afternoon thunderstorms roll in fast in the warmer months — turn on headlights with your wipers, as Florida law requires.
